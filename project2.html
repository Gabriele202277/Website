Nonlinear State Estimation via Machine Learning

This project was focused on exploring the application of machine learning in the field of earthquake engineering. The main goal was to develop models that could help in the damage assessment of structures following an earthquake. The project involved working with real-world data and deploying a combination of supervised learning techniques to achieve accurate predictions.

The models developed, including neural networks and Gaussian processes, were thoroughly evaluated using performance metrics such as accuracy and mean squared error. The final results showcased the ability of these models to handle complex datasets and provide meaningful insights for structural damage prediction.
